Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

Overview

MagicCuts is designed with privacy as a core principle. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers.

Data Collection

MagicCuts does not collect:

  • Personal information
  • Device identifiers
  • Location data
  • Usage analytics
  • Advertising identifiers

Local Data Storage

The app stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Monitored Devices: Bluetooth device identifiers (UUIDs), names you assign, and signal strength thresholds
  • App Preferences: Your configured settings

This data never leaves your device and is stored using Apple's secure SwiftData framework.

Bluetooth Usage

MagicCuts uses Bluetooth to:

  • Discover nearby Bluetooth devices
  • Measure signal strength (RSSI) of devices
  • Check if monitored devices meet your configured proximity thresholds

Bluetooth scanning is performed only when you actively use the app or when triggered by iOS Shortcuts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why can't the app find my Bluetooth device?

A: Make sure Bluetooth is enabled on your device and the target device is powered on and in range. Some devices may not broadcast their presence continuously.

Q: What do the RSSI numbers mean?

A: RSSI (Received Signal Strength Indicator) measures signal strength in dBm. Values closer to 0 indicate a stronger signal (device is closer). Typical ranges are -30 (very close) to -90 (far away).

Q: How do I use MagicCuts with Shortcuts?

A: Open the Shortcuts app, create a new shortcut, and search for "MagicCuts" actions. You can use "Is Device Nearby" to check if a monitored device meets your signal threshold.

Q: Does the app work in the background?

A: The app can check device proximity when triggered by Shortcuts, even if the app isn't open. However, continuous background scanning is limited by iOS to preserve battery.
